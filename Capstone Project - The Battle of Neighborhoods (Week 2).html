Exploring the business potential for opening a hotel in Madrid¶

1.Introduction¶

This report explores the opportunities for the opening of a new hotel in the city of Madrid.¶

Madrid is the European tourist capital with the most average expenditure per tourist with € 355 per day.

During the last 6 years, the number of foreign tourists who have visited the Community of Madrid maintains a sustained growth, well above the rest of Spanish destinations.

The city of Madrid recorded in 2019 the arrival of 10.4 million visitors (+2.1% vs 2018), of which 55% were international and 45% national. 22.6 million overnight stays were generated (+5.7% vs 2018). The main international tourism source markets were the United States, Italy, France, the United Kingdom and Germany, and the fastest growing markets were the United States, China, Italy, Brazil and Mexico. A total expense of 10,451 million was reached (+12.6% vs 2018) and an average daily expense of 270 euros (+11% vs 2018).

The number of rooms in 5-star hotels in Madrid is growing, although it is still below that of other European capitals. This represents a business opportunity in a growth environment.

In the capital there are more than 5,100 rooms available in 5-star establishments compared to almost 22,000 in Paris or more than 18,000 in London. Most of the hotels in Madrid are 4-stars, therefore this is the segment with the highest competition.

2. The data¶

The following data sets will be used for this report:¶

  • A dataset containing the districts and neighborhoods of Madrid will be used. This dataset will include the geographic coordinates of each district for subsequent location on maps and clusters. Madrid has 21 districts that, in addition, group a total of 131 administrative neighborhoods. This information will be obtained from Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Districts_of_Madrid) and from official organizations such as (http://gestiona.madrid.org/iestadis/) and (http://gestiona.madrid.org/nomecalles/DescargaBDTCorte.icm#delimitacionesterritoriales)

  • We will use Foursquare.com to obtain leisure venues that may be of interest to tourists, from each district and existing hotels. Crossing this information with the previous one of the districts we will be able to group the information obtained in clusters and we will be able to represent them on maps for a better understanding. In this way we will be able to determine the best location for the new hotel.

<p><a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, the capital of Spain, is divided into <b>21 districts</b>, which are further subdivided into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_wards_of_Madrid" title="List of wards of Madrid">131 administrative wards</a>. Additional neighborhoods exist outside the boundaries of administrative borders. Each district is governed by a body named <i>Junta Municipal de Distrito</i>. Residents of Madrid are typically called <i>Madrileños</i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Popular_neighbourhoods">Popular neighbourhoods</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Popular neighbourhoods">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bancoporlatarde.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Bancoporlatarde.jpg/220px-Bancoporlatarde.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Bancoporlatarde.jpg/330px-Bancoporlatarde.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Bancoporlatarde.jpg/440px-Bancoporlatarde.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bancoporlatarde.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Spain" title="Bank of Spain">Bank of Spain</a> building (foreground) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palacio_de_Comunicaciones" title="Palacio de Comunicaciones">Palacio de Comunicaciones</a></div></div></div>
<p>Contemporary Madrid came into its own after the death of general <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Franco" title="Francisco Franco">Francisco Franco</a>. Reaction against the dictatorial bureaucracy centered in Madrid, and a history of centralism that predated Franco by centuries has resulted in the successful modern movement towards increased <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autonomous_entity" title="Autonomous entity">autonomy</a> for the regions of Spain, considered autonomous regions under the umbrella of Spain. The modern metropolis is home to over three million people.
</p>
<h3><span id="Alonso_Mart.C3.ADnez"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Alonso_Martínez">Alonso Martínez</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Alonso Martínez">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This area contains the large <a href="/wiki/Plaza_de_Col%C3%B3n" title="Plaza de Colón">Plaza de Colón</a>. This plaza commemorates <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a>, who was responsible for ushering in the Spanish imperial golden age of the 16th and 17th centuries.  It is within walking distance of the main cultural and commercial areas of the city, such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prado" title="Prado">Prado</a> museum, the expansive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parque_del_Buen_Retiro" title="Parque del Buen Retiro">Parque del Buen Retiro</a> and the business center on the lower part of the <a href="/wiki/Paseo_de_la_Castellana" title="Paseo de la Castellana">Paseo de la Castellana</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atocha">Atocha</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Atocha">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Atocha_(Madrid)" title="Atocha (Madrid)">Atocha</a> covers a large area and is bordered by the Huertas and Lavapiés neighbourhoods. It contains several notable cultural institutions, including the <a href="/wiki/Museo_Nacional_Centro_de_Arte_Reina_Sof%C3%ADa" title="Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofía">Reina Sofía Museum</a>. Also located here is the central bus terminal and the <a href="/wiki/Madrid_Atocha_railway_station" title="Madrid Atocha railway station">Atocha Railway Station</a>, one of two main train stations in Madrid, the other being located in <a href="/wiki/Chamart%C3%ADn_(Madrid)" title="Chamartín (Madrid)">Chamartín</a>. This was the main site of the <a href="/wiki/2004_Madrid_train_bombings" title="2004 Madrid train bombings">train bombings</a> carried out on March 11, 2004. Atocha was also the site of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1977_Massacre_of_Atocha" title="1977 Massacre of Atocha">1977 Massacre of Atocha</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="AZCA_.2F_Nuevos_Ministerios"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="AZCA_/_Nuevos_Ministerios">AZCA / Nuevos Ministerios</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: AZCA / Nuevos Ministerios">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Torre_Picasso_-_01.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2700" data-file-width="1800" decoding="async" height="330" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Torre_Picasso_-_01.jpg/220px-Torre_Picasso_-_01.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Torre_Picasso_-_01.jpg/330px-Torre_Picasso_-_01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Torre_Picasso_-_01.jpg/440px-Torre_Picasso_-_01.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Torre_Picasso_-_01.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Torre Picasso, designed by <a href="/wiki/Minoru_Yamasaki" title="Minoru Yamasaki">Minoru Yamasaki</a></div></div></div>
<p>Located in the Cuatro Caminos ward, <a href="/wiki/AZCA" title="AZCA">AZCA</a> is the financial center of Madrid. The area is populated by skyscrapers, among them <a href="/wiki/Torre_Picasso" title="Torre Picasso">Torre Picasso</a> at 157 metres (515 ft), Edificio BBVA at 107 metres (351 ft) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puerta_de_Europa" title="Puerta de Europa">Torre Europa</a> at 115 metres (377 ft). The skyscraper <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Torre_Windsor" title="Torre Windsor">Torre Windsor</a> once stood here as well, until it burned completely on the night of 12 February 2005. A large <a href="/wiki/El_Corte_Ingl%C3%A9s" title="El Corte Inglés">El Corte Inglés</a> department store consisting of three interconnected buildings is also located here. The area is directly linked to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barajas_Airport" title="Barajas Airport">Barajas Airport</a> by <a href="/wiki/Madrid_Metro" title="Madrid Metro">metro</a> line 8 at the <i>Nuevos Ministerios</i> station. It's the <a href="/wiki/Parkour" title="Parkour">Parkour</a> centre of Madrid.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuatro_Torres_Business_Area">Cuatro Torres Business Area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cuatro Torres Business Area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Cuatro_Torres_Business_Area" title="Cuatro Torres Business Area">Cuatro Torres Business Area</a> is composed of the four tallest skyscrapers in Madrid. The tallest is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Torre_Bankia" title="Torre Bankia">Torre Bankia</a>, once known as the Torre <a href="/wiki/Repsol" title="Repsol">Repsol</a>, at 250 metres (820 ft). It was designed by <a href="/wiki/Norman_Foster,_Baron_Foster_of_Thames_Bank" title="Norman Foster, Baron Foster of Thames Bank">Sir Norman Foster</a>, and is the third tallest skyscraper in Europe. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Torre_de_Cristal_(Madrid)" title="Torre de Cristal (Madrid)">Torre de Cristal</a>, or 'Crystal Tower', is only 0.6 metres (2 ft 0 in) lower than Torre Bankia, at 249.4 metres (818 ft), and was designed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cesar_Pelli" title="Cesar Pelli">Cesar Pelli</a>. <a href="/wiki/Torre_PwC" title="Torre PwC">Torre PwC</a> is the third tallest in Madrid, at 235 metres (771 ft), and was designed by Enrique Alvarez &amp; Carlos Rubio. The fourth skyscraper is <a href="/wiki/Torre_Espacio" title="Torre Espacio">Torre Espacio</a>, or 'Space Tower'. It is 223 metres (732 ft) tall and it was designed by I.M. Pei.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CTBA_Madrid_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-CTBA_Madrid-3">[2]</a></sup> The four skyscrapers were finished in 2008.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chueca">Chueca</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Chueca">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Chueca" title="Chueca">Chueca</a> is well known as a <a href="/wiki/Gay_village" title="Gay village">centre of gay culture</a> in Madrid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Let's_Go:_Spain_&amp;_Portugal_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Let's_Go:_Spain_&amp;_Portugal-4">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Las_Cortes">Las Cortes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Las Cortes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This small area is notable for housing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Congress_of_Deputies_(Spain)" title="Congress of Deputies (Spain)">Congress of Deputies</a>, known as the <i>Congreso de los diputados</i>, the lower house of the Spanish parliament. Other notable sites include the <a href="/wiki/Thyssen-Bornemisza_Museum" title="Thyssen-Bornemisza Museum">Thyssen-Bornemisza Museum</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Banco_de_Espa%C3%B1a" title="Banco de España">Banco de España</a>, the Café del Círculo de Bellas Artes, the Zarzuela Theater and the <a href="/wiki/Plaza_de_Cibeles" title="Plaza de Cibeles">Plaza de Cibeles</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Huertas">Huertas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Huertas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This neighbourhood, also known as Barrio de las Letras, is west of the Paseo del Prado and north of Calle Atocha. Once the home and popular hangout of writers such as <a href="/wiki/Miguel_de_Cervantes" title="Miguel de Cervantes">Miguel de Cervantes</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Calderon_de_la_Barca" title="Calderon de la Barca">Calderon de la Barca</a>, Huertas is now considered Madrid's literary neighbourhood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Let's_Go:_Spain_&amp;_Portugal_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Let's_Go:_Spain_&amp;_Portugal-4">[3]</a></sup> One prominent landmark is the house where Cervantes died in 1616.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[4]</a></sup>  It is a popular nightlife spot and tourist destination.
</p>
<h3><span id="Gran_V.C3.ADa"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Gran_Vía">Gran Vía</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Gran Vía">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Croisement_Gan_Via-Alcala.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="506" data-file-width="800" decoding="async" height="139" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Croisement_Gan_Via-Alcala.jpg/220px-Croisement_Gan_Via-Alcala.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Croisement_Gan_Via-Alcala.jpg/330px-Croisement_Gan_Via-Alcala.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Croisement_Gan_Via-Alcala.jpg/440px-Croisement_Gan_Via-Alcala.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Croisement_Gan_Via-Alcala.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Gran Vía</div></div></div>
<p>As the name implies, the Gran Vía area contains one of Madrid's most important avenues, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gran_V%C3%ADa_(Madrid)" title="Gran Vía (Madrid)">Gran Vía</a> (literally, "Great Way"). First and foremost it is a shopping street; however, it also contains a number of tourist accommodations, plenty of nightlife (including Madrid's large <a href="/wiki/Goth_subculture" title="Goth subculture">goth scene</a>), many of Madrid's largest movie theaters and live musical shows. It is also a hub for Madrid's <a href="/wiki/Red-light_district" title="Red-light district">red-light district</a>, as it links many side streets. One of these streets, calle Fuencarral, has become a link between the old shopping area of the center of the city, the Bohemian Malasaña, and the hip Chueca district, making of it one of the most cosmopolitan areas of the city.
</p>
<h3><span id="Lavapi.C3.A9s"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Lavapiés">Lavapiés</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Lavapiés">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>One of the poorer neighbourhoods near the city center, <a href="/wiki/Lavapi%C3%A9s" title="Lavapiés">Lavapiés</a> has maintained much of true 'Madrileño' spirit of the past. Lavapiés has a high concentration of immigrants (mainly Chinese, Arabs, Hindi, Africans and Caribbeans), and also tends to attract artists and writers, adding to the cosmopolitan mix. The presence of immigrants has led to a variety of good, cheap shops and restaurants of both local and international food. It's one of the best places to find foreign herbs and spices. It is changing rapidly under the pressures of <a href="/wiki/Gentrification" title="Gentrification">gentrification</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="La_Latina">La Latina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: La Latina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Plaza_de-Espa%C3%B1a_En_Madrid.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="800" data-file-width="599" decoding="async" height="294" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/8d/Plaza_de-Espa%C3%B1a_En_Madrid.jpg/220px-Plaza_de-Espa%C3%B1a_En_Madrid.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/8d/Plaza_de-Espa%C3%B1a_En_Madrid.jpg/330px-Plaza_de-Espa%C3%B1a_En_Madrid.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/8d/Plaza_de-Espa%C3%B1a_En_Madrid.jpg/440px-Plaza_de-Espa%C3%B1a_En_Madrid.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Plaza_de-Espa%C3%B1a_En_Madrid.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Edificio España</div></div></div>
<p>In and around <a href="/wiki/Barrio_de_La_Latina_(Madrid)" title="Barrio de La Latina (Madrid)">La Latina</a> are the origins of Madrid. Its difficult to put precise boundaries on La Latina, because, like its immediate neighbours, streets are narrow and winding. There are quite a few nightlife spots, though the neighbourhood is best known as having a concentration of <a href="/wiki/Tapas" title="Tapas">tapas</a> bars (particularly on the Cava Baja and Cava Alta). There are also a number of attractive churches, including the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s_(Madrid)" title="Church of San Andrés (Madrid)">Church of San Andrés</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Francisco_el_Grande_Basilica" title="San Francisco el Grande Basilica">San Francisco el Grande Basilica</a>. On Sundays and major holidays, the famous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rastro" title="Rastro">Rastro</a> flea-market begins on the eastern edge, spilling out of the San Millan exit of the La Latina metro stop to the Plaza de Cascorro and all the way to the Ronda de Toledo to the south. Plaza de la Paja is another interesting location here. On its other side, La Latina borders Plaza Mayor and another ancient part of the town, <a href="/wiki/El_Madrid_de_los_Austrias" title="El Madrid de los Austrias">El Madrid de los Austrias</a>, where you can find the beautiful <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palacio_Real" title="Palacio Real">Palacio Real</a> (Royal Palace).
</p>
<h3><span id="Malasa.C3.B1a"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Malasaña">Malasaña</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Malasaña">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Malasa%C3%B1a" title="Malasaña">Malasaña</a> is famous for its nightlife, and for having been the main hub of the <a href="/wiki/La_Movida_Madrile%C3%B1a" title="La Movida Madrileña">movida</a> countercultural movement in the 1970s and 1980s. Considerated very popular and one of the best´s Madrid´s neighbourhoods with available pubs and bars during night.
</p>
<h3><span id=".C3.93pera"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Ópera">Ópera</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Ópera">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As the name implies, this area around the <a href="/wiki/%C3%93pera_(Madrid_Metro)" title="Ópera (Madrid Metro)">Ópera metro stop</a> is the location of Madrid's main Opera Theatre, the <a href="/wiki/Teatro_Real" title="Teatro Real">Teatro Real</a> (Royal Theatre). In front of the theatre is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palacio_Real_de_Madrid" title="Palacio Real de Madrid">Palacio Real de Madrid</a>, or Royal Palace. The Royal Palace is no longer used as a residence, but it has been kept intact, serving as a cultural monument that is occasionally used for state functions. It's one of largest palaces in Europe, surpassing even the Versailles Palace.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retiro">Retiro</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Retiro">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Retiropark.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="592" data-file-width="800" decoding="async" height="163" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/Retiropark.jpg/220px-Retiropark.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/Retiropark.jpg/330px-Retiropark.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/Retiropark.jpg/440px-Retiropark.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Retiropark.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Parque del buen Retiro</div></div></div>
<p>Barrio Retiro is a classic neighbourhood surrounding the famous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buen_Retiro_Park" title="Buen Retiro Park">Buen Retiro Park</a>, to the east of the center of the city. It is demographically the oldest neighbourhood in the city, but over the past five years the increasing number of flats for rent has been attracting young people and students to the area in growing numbers. The neighbourhood can be characterized by the style of its three most important areas. In the north, centering on Calle Ibiza, streets are in a grid pattern and buildings, normally 6-10 storeys, are packed closely together, creating a bustling atmosphere of small shops, bars, and restaurants. This area is very similar to the neighbouring Salamanca district. In the south, called Niño Jesus, the area is quieter, more spacious, and residential. In the west, the Jerónimos area, with its beautiful 18th and 19th century buildings and many museums (including the <a href="/wiki/Museo_del_Prado" title="Museo del Prado">Museo del Prado</a>), has few private residences, its large flats that often surpass 200 m² (2,150 sq ft) being mainly used for offices.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hortaleza">Hortaleza</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Hortaleza">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A residential district in the north, Hortaleza includes the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Olympic_Committee" title="Spanish Olympic Committee">Spanish Olympic Committee</a>, the <a href="/wiki/IFEMA" title="IFEMA">IFEMA</a> (Madrid's main trade fair centre) and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Carlos_I_Park" title="Juan Carlos I Park">Juan Carlos I Park</a>, a park between Campo de las Naciones (commercial district) and Barajas. Mar de Cristal is a tube station in Hortaleza district with an airport connection.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vallecas">Vallecas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Vallecas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Vallecas" title="Vallecas">Vallecas</a> is a working-class residential neighbourhood in the south of Madrid. It is also home to the <a href="/wiki/Rayo_Vallecano" title="Rayo Vallecano">Rayo Vallecano</a> football team.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aravaca">Aravaca</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Madrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Aravaca">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Aravaca" title="Aravaca">Aravaca</a> is an upper-class residential suburb in the northwest of Madrid. During the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War" title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a> the front was stabilized here for almost three years. Aravaca and its surroundings include parkland and wooded areas such as <a href="/wiki/Casa_de_Campo" title="Casa de Campo">Casa de Campo</a>.
</p>

def getNearbyVenues(names, latitudes, longitudes, radius=500, limit=100):
    
    venues_list=[]
    for name, lat, lng in zip(names, latitudes, longitudes):
        print(name)
            
        # create the API request URL
        url = 'https://api.foursquare.com/v2/venues/explore?&client_id={}&client_secret={}&v={}&ll={},{}&radius={}&limit={}'.format(
            CLIENT_ID, 
            CLIENT_SECRET, 
            VERSION, 
            lat, 
            lng, 
            radius, 
            limit)
            
        # make the GET request
        results = requests.get(url).json()["response"]['groups'][0]['items']
        
        # return only relevant information for each nearby venue
        venues_list.append([(
            name, 
            lat, 
            lng, 
            v['venue']['name'], 
            v['venue']['location']['lat'], 
            v['venue']['location']['lng'],  
            v['venue']['categories'][0]['name']) for v in results])

    nearby_venues = pd.DataFrame([item for venue_list in venues_list for item in venue_list])
    nearby_venues.columns = ['District', 
                  'District Latitude', 
                  'District Longitude', 
                  'Venue', 
                  'Venue Latitude', 
                  'Venue Longitude', 
                  'Venue Category' ]
    
    return(nearby_venues)

def return_most_common_venues(row, num_top_venues):
    row_categories = row.iloc[1:]
    row_categories_sorted = row_categories.sort_values(ascending=False)
    
    return row_categories_sorted.index.values[0:num_top_venues]

4.Results¶

Based on the information provided by Foursquare, we can see how most of the districts of Madrid are well provided with restaurants and other venues that can attract the attention of tourists.

The Barajas district is the one with the highest concentration of hotels. This makes sense, because it is the district where the international airport is located. Therefore this district should be avoided. It is also located on the outskirts of town.

Among the most central districts we can highlight two of them. Salamanca and Retiro.

Salamanca is well known for being one of the wealthiest and expensive areas in Madrid, with a high living cost and one of the highest real estate prices in the city. Serrano street is listed as the third most expensive street in Spain. A significant number of diplomatic missions are set in Salamanca district, such as the Swiss embassy at Nuñez de Balboa with Goya street, the French embassy at Villalar Street, the United States embassy at 74 Serrano street, or the Italian embassy in Juan Bravo Street.(wikipedia)

Retiro's history has been strongly determined by the presence of the park with the same name and the railway facilities located at the south and west of this District. Retiro Park, together with Paseo del Prado, has given its own personality to the area of Los Jerónimos, while the rest of the District is determined by Atocha Railway Station and previously also by Niño Jesús Station. During the 20th Century, the area consolidated as residential areas for people with a growing economic power.(wikipedia)

Both districts are well-stocked with tourist resources and have a low concentration of hotels. The biggest drawback is finding soil to build a hotel. Perhaps it would be easier to find a building to reform as a hotel.

5.Conclusion¶

Based on the analyzed data, my advice would be to focus on only two Madrid districts, Salamanca and Retiro, when investing on a new hotel. Especially being a high standing or luxury hotel.
